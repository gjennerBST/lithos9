Mission Briefing

The year is 2085. A deep-space transport ship, the Reactive Spirit, has crash-landed on a moon where the atmosphere is 100% water vapour and the surface is covered in liquid bromine. The ship was carrying three massive, unlabeled canisters of pure Group 1 metals: Metal A, Metal B, and Metal C.

During the crash, the external sensors recorded partial information before they were destroyed. Your task is to use the evidence and your knowledge of Group 1 trends to identify each metal, predict its behaviour, and recommend a safe storage solution.

Task 1 Identify Metals A, B, and C and justify each choice.
Task 2 Write the balanced chemical equation for Metal B with water.
Task 3 + 4 Explain reactivity and propose a safe storage solution.

Recovered Evidence Logs

Access the recovered files below and cross-reference the evidence before submitting your report.

Canister A Sensor log recovered

The metal was soft enough to be sliced by the ship's emergency hull-cutters. When a small piece fell into a puddle of water, it darted across the surface, fizzed violently, and eventually caught fire with a lilac flame.

Key observations

  • Soft metal
  • Very vigorous reaction with water
  • Ignites during reaction
  • Lilac flame

What this suggests

Use the trends in Group 1 and any flame test knowledge you already have to decide where this metal fits.

Canister B Thermal log recovered

This metal has the highest melting point of the three. It reacted steadily with water but did not melt into a ball or ignite.

Key observations

  • Highest melting point of the three
  • Reacts with water
  • Less vigorous than A
  • No melting into a ball

What this suggests

Think about how melting point changes down Group 1, and which metals react more gently.

Canister C Manipulator report recovered

This metal is the most "nervous". The robotic arm reported that this element has the largest atomic radius of the three and lost its outer electron with the least amount of energy required.

Key observations

  • Largest atomic radius
  • Outer electron easiest to lose
  • Most reactive of the three by atomic model

What this suggests

Use electronic configuration ideas, not just visible reaction clues, to explain why this one is most reactive.

Reference Archive // Group 1 Trends

Scientific trend archive restored from damaged onboard records.

Trend What happens down Group 1? Why it matters here
Atomic radius Increases The outer electron gets further from the nucleus.
Nuclear attraction to outer electron Decreases The outer electron is held less strongly.
Ease of losing outer electron Increases Reactivity increases down the group.
Reactivity with water Increases Reactions become more vigorous down the group.
Melting point Decreases Lower melting point metals are more likely to melt during reaction.
Softness Generally increases Metals lower in the group are easier to cut.

Useful reaction pattern

Group 1 metal + water → metal hydroxide + hydrogen

Remember to balance the equation and include state symbols.

Storage reminder

Group 1 metals react quickly with water and oxygen in air, so they must be kept away from moisture and reactive gases.
